The situation

The notice is long.
The deadline is real.
The decision is thousands of dollars.

You have 60 days from the date on your notice to elect COBRA coverage. Courts have upheld this deadline even in cases of employer error, mail delays, and personal hardship. If you miss it, you lose COBRA rights permanently.

The notice itself is typically 40–60 pages of federal regulatory language. Buried inside: your actual premium (often $700–$2,000+ per month), what's covered, when coverage starts, and what your alternatives are.

Most people skim it, panic, and either elect blindly or let the deadline pass. That's what we're here to prevent.

Common Questions

Questions people actually ask.

Is this legal advice? expand_more
No. COBRAClarity is an educational document explainer. We read your notice and translate it into plain English. We are not attorneys, insurance agents, or brokers. We do not recommend specific health plans or tell you whether to elect COBRA. For advice specific to your situation, consult a licensed professional.
How fast do I get the report? expand_more
Usually within a few minutes of uploading. If our system is busy or the file is very large, it may take up to an hour. You'll receive it at the email address you provide.
What if my notice is really complicated? expand_more
COBRA notices follow a federal format, so even long ones have standard sections. Our analysis handles 40, 60, even 80-page notices — it's actually the long ones where the plain-English summary helps most.
What happens to my document after? expand_more
It's processed to generate your report and then deleted from our pipeline. We don't sell data, we don't store notices for marketing, and we don't share anything with anyone. The only thing we retain is your email address so we can send the report.
Will you tell me which option to pick? expand_more
No — and honestly, nobody should without knowing your income, family situation, health needs, and state. What we do is explain each option clearly (COBRA, ACA marketplace, spouse plan, Medicaid) so you can make an informed choice. For personalized recommendations, healthcare.gov has a free navigator program, and most states have nonprofit health insurance assisters.
I also got a severance agreement. Can you help with that? expand_more
Yes — that's our sister service. SeveranceClarity.com does the same kind of plain-English breakdown for severance agreements, including the release language, NDA, non-compete, and benefits continuation terms. Many people use both after a layoff.
